RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

 

 

 

Meat science

National Center of Meat Quality and Safety Control, 2017-NOW

 

l   

My current research focuses on the influence of a structural meat protein, plectin, on the quality of fresh meat during postmortem aging, aiming for a better understanding of the underlying structural changes of the conversion of muscle to meat. It contains three sections: Firstly, The distribution of plectin and its colocalization with other structural proteins in pork skeletal muscle has been shown by Immunofluorescence analysis. Secondly, its degradation pattern during postmortem aging and the enzymes involved has been determined using inhibitors by western blot analysis. Finally, the contribution of plectin degradation to the changes in water holding capacity of fresh meat has been measured, and a hypothesis on the potential mechanism has been proposed. Meat products processing

 

 

 

 

 

Bachelor's degree thesis, 2017.05-2017.07

l   

Institute of Agricultural Products Processing, Jiangsu Academy of Agricultural Sciences

 

l   

The study was performed to find the optimum processing conditions for the microwave vacuum puffing of duck breast after hot-air dehydration. Firstly, the one-factor-at-a-time method was used to examine the effects of initial moisture content, microwave intensity, microwave irradiation time and vacuum degree on volume shrinkage, rehydration ratio, sensory score and oxidation of puffed duck breast. Subsequently, response surface methodology was used to optimize initial moisture content, microwave intensity, microwave irradiation time. These experimental results can provide a theoretical reference for the industrial production of puffed duck products
